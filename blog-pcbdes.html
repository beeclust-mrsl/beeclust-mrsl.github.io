Terminologies Used

  • Plated through hole: A hole on a board which has an annular ring and which is plated all the way through the board. May be a connection point for a through hole component, a via to pass a signal through, or a mounting hole.
  • Pad: A portion of exposed metal on the surface of a board to which a component is soldered (applicable to SMD Components)
  • Trace: A continuous path of copper on a circuit board. The traceâ€™s function can be visualized as the wire
  • Via: A a hole in a board used to pass a signal from one layer to another. Tented vias are covered by soldermask to protect them from being soldered to. Vias where connectors and components are to be attached are often untented (uncovered) so that they can be easily soldered.
  • Silkscreen: A the letters, number, symbols, and imagery on a circuit board. Usually only one color is available, and resolution is usually fairly low
